Relationship Reflections from my perspective

Listing my main negative and positive points from my relationship notes.

Negative

  • Once we talked about nice, funny, and serious things, but now it feels like a mask of Jasper is unveiled and you want to run away, staying disconnected.
  • Our communication can often be disrespectful and unfair. Even after I raised it many times, I asked for more understanding and respect.
  • I asked for affirmation that a situation felt unpleasant and for a listening ear later, but in the past weeks you barely showed you wanted to listen.
  • You say whatever you want anytime, but if it triggers me you duck from taking responsibility and clarifying the matter.
  • You hide and detach, yet expect me to be vulnerable. You avoid conversation by pretending to be productive or working.
  • I am overwhelmed in your life, unhappy, and I feel ignored when I try to communicate how chaos at home affects me.
  • You get upset when I try to share how the situation impacts me, and we don’t address how to make our relationship work.
  • You make me feel alone while expecting me to be there for you. I love you, but I feel alone.
  • You often give me the bare minimum and then make me feel like I am too much for you. I am expected to adjust and be more understanding.
  • When I don’t fit your schedule, I have to adjust or stay silent, while you are often overwhelmed with work, kids, and sports.
  • It feels like our relationship is too much for you, and I wonder why you want me around if your life is already hectic.
  • I want this relationship to help me grow, but lately I feel more frustrated and hurt than improving.
  • Repeated patterns include self-destruction, hiding, detaching, and asking me to understand your head without meeting me halfway.
  • You say it hurts when you leave, but when I tell you what hurts me most, you don’t manage well and leave me sleepless and hurt.
  • You can leave or shut down thinking it is best, but I keep asking if we can try something different than our usual approach.
  • I love you, but you still don’t understand how to love and take care of me, making me feel more alone than ever.
  • I’d rather find myself again, even if it means living alone.
  • I promised I wanted to work things out, but our relationship consists of both of us, and I can’t promise to stay forever if I feel alone and unheard.
  • We are different with different expectations. Your mood and fatigue make it hard for me to rely on you, and I am tired of constantly adjusting.
  • When I raise problems, you make it about yourself instead of listening, saying it’s always your fault or that I should leave if I’m unhappy.
  • The pattern is detaching and disconnecting during conflict. You said you wanted to step up, but it hasn’t happened.
  • I’m willing to stay and work through misunderstandings if we both improve communication, but it hasn’t felt that way recently.
  • Most conversations feel unbalanced, like one of us reaches out while the other pulls away. That dynamic is unhealthy.

Positive

  • I love you; being around you makes my heart happy.
  • You taught me how to love and showed me love in important ways.
  • You supported me in working on myself and encouraged me to continue therapy.
  • We had nice times together—being in the car, traveling, and creating new memories.
  • I want to take care of you and spend the rest of my life with you; caring for you makes me happy.
  • Doing mundane things together—cleaning, cooking, sitting, reading, walking—made me happy.
  • I thought you understood me and saw me for who I am.
  • When you are actually present, your small and sweet gestures become treasured memories.
  • You helped me understand what love can feel like, and I’m grateful for the ways you showed it to me.
  • I value the time we shared and the moments that made me feel close and content.
  • Even simple everyday moments with you became core memories I treasure.
